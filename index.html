Flagship research

Advanced Science · 2026

Ouroboros

A unified molecular foundation model that closes a persistent gap in molecular AI: representation models learn chemical meaning, while generators often operate in a separate space. Ouroboros reconnects them through a shared encoding that can be predicted from, optimized, and decoded.

Peer-reviewed paper ↗ Preprint ↗ Code & models ↗

01 · Encode

Compress chemical space

A global-attention GNN maps each molecular graph to a 2048-dimensional vector. Similarity learning organizes that space using molecular fingerprints, conformational space, and pharmacophore information.

02 · Navigate

Turn prediction into direction

Independent property decoders map encodings to task-specific objectives. Their gradients update vectors directly, making property models navigators for directed molecular evolution.

03 · Decode

Return to valid structures

An autoregressive Transformer reconstructs SMILES from the optimized vector. Decoupled training lets the encoder learn chemically meaningful structure without being constrained by generation.

What navigation enables

Three operations turn the encoding from a descriptor into a design space.

Exploration

Perturb a starting encoding to sample nearby, structurally diverse analogs.

Migration

Optimize solubility, permeability, or learned objectives while preserving useful similarity.

Fusion

Navigate toward multiple reference sets to propose polypharmacology candidates.

Current boundary

Direct drug–target affinity prediction is not yet part of the framework; target-specific candidates still require docking, binding-pose analysis, and experimental validation. This is the next interface between molecular and protein representation.
